#PROJECT
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	Lack of consistent effect of dietary fiber on immune checkpoint blockade
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	efficacy across diverse murine tumor models
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	Immune checkpoint blockade (ICB) has transformed cancer treatment, but success
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	rates remain limited. Recent research suggests dietary fiber enhances ICB
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	response in mice and patients through microbiome-dependent mechanisms. Yet, the
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	robustness of this effect across cancer types and dietary contexts remains
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	unclear. Specifically, prior literature compared grain-based chow (high fiber)
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	to low-fiber purified diet, but these diets differ also on other dimensions
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	including phytochemicals. Here we investigated, in mice fed grain-based chow or
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	purified diets with differing quantities of isolated fibers (cellulose and
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	inulin), the gut microbiome, metabolite levels and ICB activity in multiple
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	tumor models. Isolated fibers shifted multiple microbial taxa toward chow-like
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	levels, although diet-type (chow vs. purified) had a stronger impact on
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	gut-microbiome composition. Metabolomic profiles were relatively similar between
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	mice fed high- and low-fiber purified diets, but differed massively between mice
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	fed purified diets or chow, identifying the factor as diet type, independent of
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	fiber. Tumor growth studies in multiple murine models revealed that fiber has a
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	weaker impact on ICB (anti-PD-1) efficacy than previously reported. While diet
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	impacted ICB activity in some models, the effect was not directionally
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	consistent. In no model did we observe the pattern expected if fiber controlled
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	ICB efficacy: strong efficacy in both chow and high-fiber purified diet but low
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	efficacy in low-fiber purified diet. Thus, dietary fiber appears to have limited
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	or inconsistent effect on ICB efficacy in mouse models, and other dietary
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	factors that correlate with fiber intake may underlie the clinical correlations
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	between fiber consumption and immunotherapy outcomes.

CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	Blood was collected by tail snip in ad lib fed mice around 9am, the day after
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	the sixth dose of anti-PD-1 or isotype control. Blood was centrifuged at 10,000
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	g, 4°C for 10 min. Serum was isolated from the supernatant and stored at -80°C
CO:COLLECTION_SUMMARY            	until further analysis.

#TREATMENT
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	Mice were randomized and assigned to treatment groups when the average tumor
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	volume reached 40-100 mm3. Mice were dosed with 100ug of anti-PD-1 (BE0273,
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	clone 29F.1A12, BioXcell) or isotype control (BE0089, Rat IgG2a, BioXcell). This
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	was administered as a 100 µl intraperitoneal injection, diluted in InVivoPure
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	pH 7.0 Dilution Buffer (IP0070, BioXcell) for Anti-PD-1 or InVivoPure pH 6.5
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	Dilution Buffer (IP0065, BioXcell) for the IgG2a isotype control. Mice were
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	dosed every 3 days for a total of 8 doses.

SP:SAMPLEPREP_SUMMARY            	20 min. Supernatants were collected for LC-MS analysis.
